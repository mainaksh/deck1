Not a market Quisitive is trying to create. A market being forced into existence by regulation, capital flows, and the economics of sovereign compute.

01 · Regulatory
Compliance-driven procurement cycle

Hyperscaler India regions satisfy data-residency in principle, but on-premise delivers material cost, latency, and key-management advantages for sensitive workloads.
02 · Capital

03 · Cost
Sovereign compute economics
Cloud egress charges Rs 7–8/GB structurally penalize cloud-native Indian SaaS at scale. GPU availability remains tight on public clouds; reserved-capacity pricing often exceeds list; spot instances unreliable for training workloads. On-premise HPC has become price-competitive with cloud for predictable, steady-state workloads — particularly where data residency, latency, and egress are material.

Senior HPC talent reachable in NCR
NCR has deep enterprise-IT veterans exiting Atos, TCS, HCL, BT for meaningful equity. IIT Delhi, IIIT-D, NSUT, DTU graduate 2000+ ML/CS students/year. Return-from-abroad scientists (US postdocs, UK biotech) accepting Rs 1.5–2.5 L PhD roles for scientific autonomy + ESOP.
A narrowing window. The buyers are forming procurement panels now.

Problem
Wet-lab ADMET is slow and expensive
Indian pharma runs 10–50 NCE programs in parallel. ADMET liability testing costs Rs 2–5 L per candidate, takes 6–8 weeks. At 100 candidates per program, this is Rs 2–5 Cr of sunk work that often eliminates 60%+ of the library. 30–45% of late-stage clinical failures trace back to ADMET issues predictable in silico.
Solution
GNN-based ADMET + virtual screening + MD
We dock 104–106 compounds in days, predict hERG / CYP450 / BBB / solubility per compound via DeepChem GNNs, and validate the top 10–50 hits with 100–500 ns MD simulations. Rs 2–5 L of wet-lab becomes Rs 10–30 K of compute. Delivered in 2–5 days.
Indian CRDMO gross margins are 40–50%. Computational services support 55–70% gross.
Because we replace labor-intensive screening with software-replicable computation, and our infrastructure is a sunk cost amortized across three verticals. Syngene FY24 illustrates the comparison: ~45% gross / ~28% EBITDA. Our Y3 target: 60–65% gross, 30–40% EBITDA at scale.

The Tailwind

Four Indian regulators are forcing this market into existence.

Quisitive is not betting on market adoption. We are positioning against compliance deadlines that are already law and have funded enforcement mechanisms behind them.

RBI
IT Governance + Fraud Risk Master Directions (2023–2024)
RBI Master Direction on IT Governance (RBI/2023-24/107, Nov 2023) + Master Direction on Fraud Risk Management (2024) + Cyber Resilience MD for non-bank PSOs (July 2024) mandate Board-approved cyber policy, 24×7 SOC, continuous surveillance, early fraud detection. AI-based detection explicitly encouraged (RBI HaRBInger initiative). Enforcement inspection cycle 2025–2027 drives procurement urgency. Affects scheduled banks, NBFCs (upper & middle layer), and fintechs. ForgeShield + ForgeSOC directly address this regulatory expectation.
DPDP Act 2023
Data Protection Act operationalized
All data fiduciaries (essentially every Indian enterprise handling personal data) must implement technical and organizational safeguards, sensitive-category localization, and breach notification. Penalties up to Rs 250 Cr per significant incident. ForgeVault + ForgeIdentity + Managed Services SaaS Hosting directly address compliance needs.
SEBI CSCRF
Cybersecurity framework for regulated entities (2024)
SEBI’s Cybersecurity & Cyber Resilience Framework applies to SEBI-regulated entities — stock brokers, mutual funds, depositories, KRAs, RTAs — mandating comprehensive controls, 24×7 SOC, VAPT, incident response. Periodic attestations required. ForgeSOC + compliance bundle map to the framework’s control areas.

The BIRAC RDI Fund issued its first national call for proposals on 13 February 2026, actively soliciting deep-tech proposals.
